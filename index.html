An Apothecary's Guide: Lesson 277: Mycology

Welcome to our humble guide for aspiring apothecaries!

For this lesson, we will talk about mushrooms!

While there are many types of mushrooms in the arboretum, it would help to know what each one does!

Generally, avoid mushrooms with green on them. Those ones are... volitile.

Ones with red on them are superfoods, anything that feeds on them will get real strong real quick.

Ones with white on them are generally average.

Ones with blue on them make for great fertilizers!

Purple ones have some strange effects, so don't touch those!

Yellow ones have a whole lot of vitamin D in them!

And Orange ones taste like oranges!


Our state-of-the-art mushroom farms makes sure that there will always be at least one of each type of mushroom in the farms at once, though how it works is a little iffy.


If you plan on making fertilizer, well, I have a killer fertilizer recipe for you!

But first, I'd have to explain how it works.

When I made this fertilizer, I came up with a color point system.

Essentially, you make the fertilizer based on the mushroom colors!

The mushroom's main color provides 2 points, while the color of the spots give one point.

For example, a white mushroom with red spots would give 2 white points and 1 red point.

This ultimate fertilizer of mine requires...

10 blue points.

1 white point.

7 red points.

5 yellow points.

And 3 orange points.
